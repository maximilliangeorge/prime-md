# The general form of truth-function

The general form of truth-function is: [p̄, ξ̄, N(ξ̄)]. (6)

This says nothing else than that every proposition is the result of successive
applications of the operation N(ξ̄) to the elementary propositions. (6.001) If
[all propositions are truth-functions of elementary
propositions](./truth-functions.md), then there must be a single operation whose
iterated application generates every possible proposition.

That operation is joint negation (the Sheffer stroke generalized to any number
of arguments). From it and the elementary propositions, the whole of logic
follows. The propositions of logic are tautologies. (6.1) They say
nothing. (6.11) They are the analytical propositions.

Logic is not a theory but a reflexion of the world. (6.13) Logic is
transcendental. (6.13)
