# Propositions are truth-functions of elementary propositions

Propositions are truth-functions of elementary propositions. (5) An elementary
proposition is a truth-function of itself. (5)

If [the simplest propositions assert atomic facts](./elementary-propositions.md),
then every complex proposition is built from these by truth-functional
combination. The truth-possibilities of the elementary propositions are the
conditions of the truth and falsehood of propositions. (4.41)

The truth-functions can be ordered in series. That is the foundation of the
theory of probability. (5.1) All truth-functions are results of the successive
application of a finite number of truth-operations to elementary
propositions. (5.3)

Among the possible groups of truth-conditions there are two extreme cases:
tautology (true for all truth-possibilities) and contradiction (false for
all). (4.46) Tautology and contradiction are without sense — they say
nothing. (4.461) But they are not nonsensical; they are part of the symbolism,
as "0" is part of arithmetic. (4.4611)
