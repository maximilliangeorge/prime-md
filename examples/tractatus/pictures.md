# We make to ourselves pictures of facts

We make to ourselves pictures of facts. (2.1) The picture presents the facts in
logical space, the existence and non-existence of atomic facts. (2.11) The
picture is a model of reality. (2.12)

Because [reality is constituted by atomic facts](./atomic-facts.md), a picture
must represent possible combinations of objects. And because [every picture must
share logical form with what it depicts](./logical-form.md), the picture is
itself a fact — its elements are combined with one another in a definite
way. (2.14, 2.141)

The form of representation is the possibility that things are combined with one
another as are the elements of the picture. (2.151) Thus the picture is linked
with reality; it reaches up to it. (2.1511) It is like a scale applied to
reality. (2.1512)

The picture agrees with reality or not; it is right or wrong, true or
false. (2.21) What the picture represents is its sense. (2.221) In the
agreement or disagreement of its sense with reality, its truth or falsity
consists. (2.222) There is no picture which is *a priori* true. (2.225)
