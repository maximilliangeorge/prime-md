# The world is the totality of facts, not of things

The world is everything that is the case. (1)

It is the totality of facts, not of things. (1.1) The world is determined by
the facts, and by these being *all* the facts. (1.11) For the totality of facts
determines both what is the case, and also all that is not the case. (1.12)

The facts in logical space are the world. (1.13) The world divides into
facts. (1.2) Any one can either be the case or not be the case, and everything
else remain the same. (1.21)

This is the ontological starting point: the fundamental units of the world are
not objects or substances, but facts — that such-and-such is the case.
