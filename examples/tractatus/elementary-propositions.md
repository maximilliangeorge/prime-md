# The simplest proposition asserts the existence of an atomic fact

The simplest proposition, the elementary proposition, asserts the existence of
an atomic fact. (4.21)

If [propositions are pictures of reality](./propositions.md), and [reality is
constituted by atomic facts](./atomic-facts.md), then there must be a most basic
kind of proposition that directly pictures a single atomic fact. This is the
elementary proposition.

The elementary proposition consists of names. It is a connexion, a
concatenation, of names. (4.22) In the analysis of propositions we must come to
elementary propositions, which consist of names in immediate combination. (4.221)

No elementary proposition can contradict another. (4.211) If the elementary
proposition is true, the atomic fact exists; if it is false the atomic fact does
not exist. (4.25) The specification of all true elementary propositions describes
the world completely. (4.26)
