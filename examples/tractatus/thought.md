# The logical picture of the facts is the thought

The logical picture of the facts is the thought. (3)

If [we make pictures of facts, and every picture is a logical
picture](./pictures.md), then there is a privileged kind of picture: the
thought. A thought is a picture whose form of representation is the logical
form itself.

"An atomic fact is thinkable" — means: we can imagine it. (3.001) The totality
of true thoughts is a picture of the world. (3.01) The thought contains the
possibility of the state of affairs which it thinks. What is thinkable is also
possible. (3.02)

We cannot think anything unlogical, for otherwise we should have to think
unlogically. (3.03) It used to be said that God could create everything, except
what was contrary to the laws of logic. The truth is, we could not say of an
"unlogical" world how it would look. (3.031)
