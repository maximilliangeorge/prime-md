# Ethics is transcendental

All propositions are of equal value. (6.4)

The sense of the world must lie outside the world. In the world everything is as
it is and happens as it does happen. *In it* there is no value — and if there
were, it would be of no value. (6.41)

If [all propositions are truth-functions](./truth-functions.md), then every
proposition pictures a possible state of affairs, and no state of affairs is
intrinsically higher or lower than another. And if [language cannot reach beyond
its own limits](./limits-of-language.md), then value cannot be expressed in
propositions.

Hence there can be no ethical propositions. (6.42) Propositions cannot express
anything higher. (6.42)

It is clear that ethics cannot be expressed. Ethics is transcendental. (6.421)
(Ethics and aesthetics are one.) (6.421)
