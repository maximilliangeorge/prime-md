# Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent

Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent. (7)

If [the limits of language are the limits of the world](./limits-of-language.md),
and if [the very propositions that brought us to see this must be thrown
away](./the-ladder.md), then all that remains is silence — not as defeat, but
as the proper response to what lies beyond the reach of sense.

This is the final proposition. It mirrors the preface: "What can be said at all
can be said clearly; and whereof one cannot speak thereof one must be silent."
The whole book lies between these two utterances of the same thought — the
first as promise, the last as verdict.
